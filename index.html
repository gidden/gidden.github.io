I'm Matthew Gidden.

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Nuclear Engineering at the University of Wisconsin - Madison, and work as a core developer of Cyclus specializing in agent-based techniques and optimization. I'm interested in many topics, including energy policy, sustainability, nuclear nonproliferation, and programming in general. Let's start scrolling and learn more about me.

About Me

I grew up in southern Louisiana, and was afforded the opportunity to study Nuclear Engineering at Texas A&M for my undergraduate education. I was able to explore a number of research opportunities during my tenure, including at Oak Ridge National Lab, Pacific Northwest National Lab, and at TN International in Paris, France. After a semester of graduate school at UW, I was selected for a 6-month stagiaire in the core design group at AREVA, also in Paris, France. Upon returning stateside, I focused my studies on optimization techniques, including linear, integer, and dynamic programming. Through my research, I have honed my programming skills, specializing in C++ and Python (and CMake; oh build systems..). I'm proud to say that I'm very active in the American Nuclear Society, sitting on a number of committees. My other primary professional focus over the past few years has been the Nuclear Engineering Student Delegation, of which I was honored to serve as a member, co-chair, and chair.
